### Slide 3: Our Solution ‚Äî ADHARA (60 seconds)

**Title:** ADHARA ‚Äî Early Learning Friction Detection

**Definition:**
> ADHARA observes *how* students interact with learning tasks ‚Äî not just *what* they answer ‚Äî to surface early signs of struggle.

**What We Detect:**

| Friction Type | What We Observe | Example |
|---------------|-----------------|---------|
| Reading Friction | Re-reading, slow progress | Student reads same line 5 times |
| Attention Friction | Long pauses, idle time | 2-minute gap mid-task |
| Memory Friction | Repeated same errors | Wrong answer ‚Üí same wrong answer |

**What We DON'T Do:**
- ‚ùå No diagnosis
- ‚ùå No medical labels
- ‚ùå No camera/biometrics
- ‚úÖ Just behavioral patterns for human review

**Speaker Notes:**
- Emphasize "learning friction" NOT "learning disability"
- Stress that humans make decisions, not the system
- This is your core differentiator ‚Äî spend time here

## üé§ Presentation Tips

### Before You Present

- [ ] Decide who speaks for each slide
- [ ] Practice transitions between speakers
- [ ] Time yourselves (aim for 5-6 minutes, leave 1-2 for questions)
- [ ] Have one person control slides

### While Presenting

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Make eye contact with judges | Read from slides |
| Pause after key points | Rush through |
| Use "we" language | Blame or make excuses |
| Acknowledge limitations honestly | Overclaim capabilities |

### Handling Questions

**Common Questions & Answers:**

**Q: "How is this different from existing LMS analytics?"**
> A: "LMS tools show you completion rates and grades ‚Äî the WHAT. ADHARA shows you behavioral patterns ‚Äî the HOW and WHY. We catch struggles before grades reveal them."

**Q: "Isn't this just predicting learning disabilities?"**
> A: "No. We explicitly avoid diagnosis. We detect friction patterns and always require human review. A teacher decides what the friction means ‚Äî we just surface it."

**Q: "What about privacy?"**
> A: "For this demo, we use only synthetic data. In a real deployment, we'd follow DPDP Act guidelines and require explicit consent."

**Q: "Does this actually work?"**
> A: "This is a prototype demonstrating the concept. The detection logic uses rule-based thresholds, not trained ML, so it's transparent and adjustable."

**Q: "What's next after the hackathon?"**
> A: "We'd love to pilot with Kerala's KITE platform to test with real (anonymized) interaction data and validate our friction thresholds."
